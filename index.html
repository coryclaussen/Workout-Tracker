Home or Gym Switch

The switch at the top of The Plan tells the tracker where you are today. On Home, any weight tip that needs more than your 45 lb dumbbells shows a red flag — do that move with the barbell, a kettlebell, or save it for Rapid Fitness. On Gym, no flags — you've got the full rack and cables.

How the Weight Tips Work

First time on a move: it shows a start range. Pick a weight inside it where 8 reps feels solid.

After that: the tip reads your last logged set. Hit the top reps? Add weight. Middle of the range? Hold and build reps. The tip is a guide — your body has the final say.

The check: the last 2 reps should be hard but your form stays clean. That's the right weight.

The Game Plan

Weeks 1–2: Learn it. Light to medium weight. Get the moves right.

Weeks 3–6: Add weight. The last 2 reps should feel hard.

Weeks 7–10: Push harder. Add a 4th day any week you have time.

Weeks 11–12: Heaviest weights of the plan.

Week 13: Easy week, then weigh in and see how far you came.

Food — Keep It Simple

  • Eat protein at every meal. Eggs, meat, Greek yogurt.
  • Stop eating 2–3 hours before bed. Late-night food is the leak.
  • Half your plate is veggies.
  • Drink water before coffee in the morning.
  • Don't skip meals on hard work days. Under-eating stalls you too.

The Truth

The gym builds the arms and shoulders. The food drops the 22 lbs that lets you see them. You need both. Three short workouts you actually do beats five you skip.
