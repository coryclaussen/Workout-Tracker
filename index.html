Logging Your Sets

Tap Log Sets under any move. Put the weight and reps for each set. Dumbbells/kettlebells: log one (45 in each hand = 45). Barbell: log the total, bar included (the bar is 45 lbs).

The weight tip averages all your sets. If your last set drops far below the rest, you'll also see a gold note — that weight may be a touch heavy.

Home or Gym Switch

The switch at the top of The Plan tells the tracker where you are. On Home, any tip past your 45 lb dumbbells shows a red flag — use the barbell, a kettlebell, or Rapid Fitness. On Gym, no flags.

How the Weight Tips Work

First time on a move: it shows a start range. Pick a weight where 8 reps feels solid.

After that: it averages your sets. Average hits the top reps? Add weight. Mid-range? Hold and build reps. The tip is a guide — your body has the final say.

Backup Your Data

Tap Export to save a backup file of all your logs. Do this once a week, or before a phone update. Lost your data? Tap Import and pick that file to get it all back.

The Game Plan

Weeks 1–2: Learn it. Light to medium weight. Get the moves right.

Weeks 3–6: Add weight. The last 2 reps should feel hard.

Weeks 7–10: Push harder. Add a 4th day any week you have time.

Weeks 11–12: Heaviest weights of the plan.

Week 13: Easy week, then weigh in and see how far you came.

Food — Keep It Simple

  • Eat protein at every meal. Eggs, meat, Greek yogurt.
  • Stop eating 2–3 hours before bed. Late-night food is the leak.
  • Half your plate is veggies.
  • Drink water before coffee in the morning.
  • Don't skip meals on hard work days. Under-eating stalls you too.

The Truth

The gym builds the arms and shoulders. The food drops the 22 lbs that lets you see them. You need both. Three short workouts you actually do beats five you skip.
